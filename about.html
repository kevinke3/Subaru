About Subaru

Engineering Excellence Since 1953

Our Heritage

Subaru began as an aircraft research company in 1917, founded by Chikuhei Nakajima. After World War II, the company shifted its focus to automobile manufacturing and introduced its first car, the Subaru 1500, in 1954.

The name "Subaru" is the Japanese word for the Pleiades star cluster, which is represented in the company's logo with six stars symbolizing the five companies that merged to create Fuji Heavy Industries and Fuji Heavy Industries itself.

Our Philosophy

At Subaru, we believe in "Confidence in Motion." This philosophy drives our commitment to engineering vehicles that provide safety, reliability, and performance in all conditions.

We focus on building cars that enhance the connection between driver, vehicle, and road while prioritizing safety and environmental responsibility.

Innovation & Technology

Subaru revolutionized the automotive industry with the introduction of the Subaru Leone 4WD Estate Van in 1972, bringing all-wheel-drive technology to mass-market passenger vehicles.

Our commitment to innovation continues with advanced safety systems like EyeSight®, the Subaru Global Platform, and our pioneering work in hybrid and electric vehicle technology.

Global Impact

With manufacturing plants in Japan and the United States, Subaru vehicles are sold in over 100 countries worldwide. Our Lafayette, Indiana plant is the first zero-landfill automotive factory in the United States.

Subaru's commitment to environmental stewardship extends beyond our manufacturing processes to support conservation efforts and outdoor recreation through our "Subaru Loves the Earth" initiative.
