Rally Racing Heritage

Subaru's rally racing heritage is legendary. From the early days of the World Rally Championship to modern rallycross competitions, Subaru drivers have pushed the limits of performance and showcased the capabilities of Subaru's symmetrical all-wheel drive system in the most challenging conditions imaginable.

Colin McRae, the "Flying Scot," became a rally legend driving for the Subaru World Rally Team from 1991 to 1998. His aggressive driving style and never-give-up attitude made him one of the most popular drivers in WRC history.

In 1995, McRae became the youngest-ever World Rally Champion at age 27, piloting the iconic Subaru Impreza 555. His partnership with co-driver Nicky Grist produced some of the most memorable moments in rally history, including his dramatic victory at the 1997 Acropolis Rally.

McRae's legacy extends beyond his championship, as he helped popularize rally racing globally and inspired a generation of drivers. His commitment to pushing limits and spectacular car control in the Subaru Impreza WRC car remain legendary in motorsport history.

"If in doubt, flat out."

- Colin McRae

Richard Burns was the first Englishman to win the World Rally Championship, achieving this feat in 2001 driving for Subaru. Known for his smooth, calculated driving style and meticulous preparation, Burns was the perfect counterpart to his more aggressive teammate Colin McRae.

Burns joined Subaru in 1999 and quickly established himself as a championship contender. His 2001 championship victory came down to the final round in Great Britain, where he secured the title in dramatic fashion. His technical understanding of car setup and consistent performances made him one of the most respected drivers of his era.

Tragically, Burns' career was cut short when he was diagnosed with a brain tumor in 2003. He passed away in 2005, but his legacy lives on through the Richard Burns Foundation, which raises money for cancer research and supports young drivers.

"Consistency is everything in rallying."

- Richard Burns

Petter "Hollywood" Solberg brought Norwegian flair and spectacular driving to the Subaru World Rally Team from 2000 to 2008. His championship victory in 2003 made him the first Norwegian to win the WRC title and marked Subaru's last manufacturers' championship.

Solberg's energetic personality and commitment to entertaining fans made him immensely popular. His dramatic sideways style and commitment to maximum attack produced some of the most exciting rally footage in history. The 2003 season saw Solberg win four rallies and consistently finish on the podium to secure the championship.

After Subaru's withdrawal from WRC, Solberg continued to compete in World Rallycross, winning the inaugural championship in 2014. He remains actively involved in motorsport through his team, Solberg World Rally Team, and continues to promote Subaru's racing heritage.

"I always drive with my heart."

- Petter Solberg

Travis Pastrana brought Subaru rallying to mainstream American audiences through his incredible stunt driving, multiple Rally America championships, and X Games success. As the face of Subaru's American rally efforts since 2003, Pastrana has won four consecutive Rally America championships from 2006 to 2009.

Pastrana's background in motocross and freestyle motocross (winning 11 X Games gold medals) gave him a unique perspective on vehicle control. His ability to adapt these skills to rally racing resulted in spectacular, crowd-pleasing performances. He founded Subaru Rally Team USA and has been instrumental in developing young American rally talent.

Beyond rally, Pastrana has set numerous world records in stunt driving with Subaru vehicles, including the longest rally jump and multiple Guinness World Records. His partnership with Subaru extends to the popular "Pastranathon" YouTube series and Nitro Rallycross championship, where he continues to showcase Subaru's performance capabilities.

"If you're not living on the edge, you're taking up too much space."

Subaru World Rally Team Legacy

1990-1994: Early Years

Subaru entered WRC with the Legacy RS, achieving its first podium in 1990. The partnership with Prodrive began in 1990, laying the foundation for future success.

1995-1999: Golden Era

The iconic blue and gold Subaru Impreza 555 dominated WRC, winning three consecutive manufacturers' championships (1995-1997) and two drivers' championships.
